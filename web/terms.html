1. What This Service Is

NOUS Token ("the Protocol", "we", "us") operates a transparent API gateway at gateway.nousai.cc, a dashboard at token.nousai.cc, and a set of smart contracts on the Base blockchain. The Protocol provides infrastructure for recording, verifying, and inscribing AI usage data on-chain.

The gateway proxies your API requests to AI providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, etc.) and records usage metadata — model name, token count, and wallet address. It does not read, store, log, or retain the content of your requests or responses.

The gateway signs cryptographic usage receipts, anchors Merkle roots on the Base blockchain, and enables minting of ERC-721 tokens ("Token-20 Proofs") representing verified records of AI consumption.

2. Nature of Token-20 Proofs

Token-20 Proofs are cryptographic records of historical AI token consumption. They are not securities, investment contracts, financial instruments, or investment products of any kind.

Each Token-20 Proof represents a verified fact: that a specific quantity of AI tokens was consumed, by a specific wallet, using a specific model, at a specific block number, as signed by the gateway and anchored in a Merkle root on the Base blockchain.

Token-20 Proofs:

  • Record past consumption — they do not entitle holders to future income, revenue, dividends, or profits of any kind
  • Do not represent equity, debt, ownership, or any claim on any entity's assets or earnings
  • Do not confer governance rights over the Protocol
  • Are not redeemable for fiat currency, cryptocurrency, or any other consideration from the Protocol
  • May have no monetary value and should not be acquired with an expectation of profit

3. Fee Structure

The Protocol charges the following fees, denominated in USDC:

  • Series Deployment Fee: A fixed fee paid to the Protocol when a Series Creator deploys a new Series. This is a one-time infrastructure fee for on-chain deployment.
  • Protocol Fee: A fixed fee of 1 USDC per inscription, paid to the Protocol. This is a flat service fee for cryptographic signing, Merkle anchoring, and on-chain verification infrastructure.
  • Creator Fee: An amount set independently by the Series Creator, paid entirely and directly to the Series Creator. The Protocol does not receive any portion of the Creator Fee. The Creator Fee is a transaction between you and the Series Creator — the Protocol is not a party to that transaction.

All fees are collected via USDC at the time of inscription, either through direct transfer (ERC-20 transferFrom) or gasless authorization (EIP-3009 transferWithAuthorization). In the EIP-3009 flow, USDC may pass through the smart contract within a single atomic transaction before being distributed to the treasury and creator. Outside of this momentary pass-through, the contract does not custody, pool, or hold user funds.

9. Smart Contract Administration

The Token20 smart contract is administered by an owner address. In the interest of transparency, the following administrative functions exist and may be exercised by the contract owner:

  • Pause / Unpause: The owner may temporarily halt all deployments and inscriptions. This is a safety mechanism intended for use only in emergencies (e.g., a discovered vulnerability).
  • Treasury Address: The owner may change the address that receives protocol fees. This is not immutable — however, any change is recorded on-chain via the TreasuryChanged event and is publicly auditable.
  • Gateway Registration: The owner may add or remove gateway addresses authorized to sign receipts and submit anchors. Revoking a gateway means future receipts from that gateway will not be accepted by the contract.
  • Anchor Invalidation: The owner may invalidate an anchored period. This is a safety mechanism intended for use only if a gateway submits a fraudulent or erroneous Merkle root. Invalidation does not affect already-minted NFTs.
  • Fee Adjustment: The owner may change the deploy fee and minimum inscription fee. The protocol fee (1 USDC) is a constant and cannot be changed by anyone. Fee changes do not apply retroactively — they affect only future transactions.
  • Ownership Transfer: The owner may transfer ownership to a new address. Ownership cannot be renounced (this is intentionally disabled to prevent the contract from becoming unmanageable).

All administrative actions emit on-chain events and are verifiable by anyone. The contract source code is publicly available and verified on Basescan.
